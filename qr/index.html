We teach ethical hacking and defense through hands‑on training, CTFs, and real threat research. We also partner with campus orgs, nonprofits, and small businesses to raise their security baseline. Below are quick QR-safety checks, then ways to plug into WRAVEN.

⚠️ Be cautious: Attackers use QR codes to drive credential theft and malware installs.
▩

QR safety tips

Check the domain. Tap-and-hold to preview the full URL. Watch for typos and weird subdomains.
Look for HTTPS + known brand. Don’t sign in on sites you don’t recognize.
Avoid installing apps from QR codes. Go to the official app store instead.
When in doubt, don’t submit. Close the page; find the destination manually.

What is WRAVEN?

WRAVEN (Western Research Advisory for Vulnerabilities, Exploits, & Networks) is Western Michigan University’s premier cybersecurity nonprofit and student organization. We teach ethical hacking & defending, run workshops and labs, compete as ECX Inc., publish research, and collaborate with nonprofits and small businesses to strengthen our community’s security.

Programs snapshot

Hands‑on Workshops. Beginner to advanced labs on web, cloud, forensics, reversing, and defense.
CTF Team — ECX Inc. Learn fast by doing. Compete with us and build practical skills.
Research & Publishing. Track active threats, writeups, and build public tools.
Community Defense. Partner with student orgs, nonprofits, and small businesses.
